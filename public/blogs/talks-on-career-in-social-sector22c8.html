Talks on “Career in Social Sector” - Another initiative by Team AtmaNirbhar

September 9, 2020 Sunil Nanda

On 16th August 2020, Team AtmaNirbhar organised the first talk on 'Career in Social Sector' by Mr Sasi Kumar. Mr Sasi Kumar, an alumnus of BITS Pilani and a Trustee member of the PARC family, had a career spanning 30+ years in the social service sector, having worked in different parts of the world including Sudan, Bangladesh, Israel, Nepal, etc., with prestigious organizations like Nossal Institute, Oxfam and United Nations Office of Drugs and Crime (UNODC).

The virtual talk was attended by around 35 students from Team AtmaNirbhar, Nirmaan, NSS and Enactus from across the Pilani, Goa and Hyderabad campuses. The session was very interactive and the participants asked a lot of questions to have a better understanding about a career in the social sector and the associated challenges, financial compensation and security.

The talk was very inspiring and thought-provoking. The speaker gave the students an idea of how one should start on their journey in the social sector and what should motivate and drive them to do their best. He also elaborated on challenges that one may face and finding methods to solve them as the most exciting parts. In addition to sharing his experience in refugee camps and working with people having serious health diseases, he guided the students on how to get support and funding for social projects and initiatives. He also created an awareness among the participants about the major organisations involved in social work. He concluded the session by saying that, like in a corporate world, one should do what makes them happy, and create a positive impact on his own life and the lives of society at large.

Another talk on the same topic organised by Team AtmaNirbhar on 22nd August 2020 featured Mr  Shobhit Mathur,  an alumnus of IIT Bombay, University of Washington and the Indian School of Business. After having worked in the North and South America in the corporate sector, Mr Mathur has spent the last 10 years in the social sector in India and is currently the Co-founder and Dean of the Rashtram School of Public Leadership.

This virtual talk also attracted abut 45 student participants for BITS campuses in Pilani, Goa and Hyderabad.

The speaker drew the attention of the students with a narrative of his own experience in moving from a high level academic background and corporate world to social and public service. He went on to explain the different categories of social/public service, associated risks and rewards and the motivating factors to remain relevant in the chosen public/social sector. He emphasised that each individual will have to have his own self-motivation and desire to achieve excellence in public life regardless of who provides the spark to his journey.

He also spoke about the balance needed between social purpose and profitability in any CSR activity. He emphasised that ultimate success and impact of any social venture or effort should be measured in terms of how many lives were impacted positively rather than what target was met.             

The talk was followed by a short question and answer session.

Both these talks, coming in quick succession, without doubt benefitted the students to consider if a career choice in social sector is exciting and worth attempting. PARC will continue to provide the grooming and nurturing for the interested students.
