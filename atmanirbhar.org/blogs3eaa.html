Talks on “Career in Social Sector” - Another initiative by Team AtmaNirbhar

Sunil Nanda
September 9, 2020

On 16th August 2020, Team AtmaNirbhar organised the first talk on 'Career in Social Sector' by Mr Sasi Kumar. Mr Sasi Kumar, an alumnus of BITS Pilani and a Trustee member of the PARC family, had a career spanning 30+ years in the social service sector, having worked in different parts of the world including Sudan, Bangladesh, Israel, Nepal, etc., with prestigious organizations like Nossal Institute, Oxfam and United Nations Office of Drugs and Crime (UNODC).

The virtual talk was attended by around 35 students from Team AtmaNirbhar, Nirmaan, NSS and Enactus from across the Pilani, Goa and Hyderabad campuses. The session was very interactive and the participants asked a lot of questions to have a better understanding about a career in the social sector and the associated challenges, financial compensation and security.

The talk was very inspiring and thought-provoking. The speaker gave the students an idea of how one should start on their journey in the social sector and what should motivate and drive them to do their best. He also elaborated on challenges that one may face and finding methods to solve them as the most exciting parts. In addition to sharing his experience in refugee camps and working with people having serious health diseases, he guided the students on how to get support and funding for social projects and initiatives. He also created an awareness among the participants about the major organisations involved in social work. He concluded the session by saying that, like in a corporate world, one should do what makes them happy, and create a positive impact on his own life and the lives of society at large.

Another talk on the same topic organised by Team AtmaNirbhar on 22nd August 2020 featured Mr  Shobhit Mathur,  an alumnus of IIT Bombay, University of Washington and the Indian School of Business. After having worked in the North and South America in the corporate sector, Mr Mathur has spent the last 10 years in the social sector in India and is currently the Co-founder and Dean of the Rashtram School of Public Leadership.

This virtual talk also attracted abut 45 student participants for BITS campuses in Pilani, Goa and Hyderabad.

The speaker drew the attention of the students with a narrative of his own experience in moving from a high level academic background and corporate world to social and public service. He went on to explain the different categories of social/public service, associated risks and rewards and the motivating factors to remain relevant in the chosen public/social sector. He emphasised that each individual will have to have his own self-motivation and desire to achieve excellence in public life regardless of who provides the spark to his journey.

He also spoke about the balance needed between social purpose and profitability in any CSR activity. He emphasised that ultimate success and impact of any social venture or effort should be measured in terms of how many lives were impacted positively rather than what target was met.             

The talk was followed by a short question and answer session.

Both these talks, coming in quick succession, without doubt benefitted the students to consider if a career choice in social sector is exciting and worth attempting. PARC will continue to provide the grooming and nurturing for the interested students.

COVID-19 Awareness Drive @ PARC 23-Jun-2020

Sunil Nanda
June 24, 2020

As a part of Covid 19 Awareness Drive, a program was organised in Pilani Atmanirbhar Resource Center (PARC), Pilani,  in collaboration with Jhunjhunu District Consumer Samiti. In this program major emphasis was given on awareness of the masses and especially how to protect self against Covid-19 infection.

The residents of Gyan Vihar and Hari Nagar participated in the event. The program was presided over by Shri Prabhu Sharan Tiwari, District Consumer samiti, Jhunjhunu and addressed by Shri Shankar Lal Maharania, Brand Ambassador, Awareness Drive, Captain Kanhaiya Lal, Dr. Ranjan Sharma, Mrs Pramila Sharma and Babulal Dhodharia. The Chief Guest of the function was Dr. V.K. Dube, Director, PARC. Dr. Dube in his informative address focused on the need for social distancing, sanitization and regular use of masks for self protection. He emphatically mentioned that strong immunity with precaution and self protection is the cure for the disease. We should learn to live with this virus without disturbing our routine life. However, in the process we should not forget to take all precautions and follow instructions by Health Officials and Government of India.  We should not hesitate in informing Health Officials about the sickness if occurs. This program was organised at the initiative of Government of Rajasthan and Government of India under the head, Covid 19 Awareness Drive.

The program was concluded with free distribution of masks to the residents of Hari Nagar and Gyan Vihar. Masks were sponsored by Stailer Nano Chem Industries, Jhunjhunu. The masks were manufactured with anti viral and anti bacterial chemicals as claimed by the industry participants.

RESPIMATIC 100 - A Low Cost Respirator

Sunil Nanda
April 16, 2020

Serendipity by Design

Three days for three iterations of a new respirator design starting from scratch with a geographically dispersed team! Another week for three iterations of working prototypes all of which have been under continuous testing for the past few days! All this under strict lockdown orders during the COVID-19 pandemic. How is this possible when it takes over 100 man-years to design full featured ICU ventilators? Here is the rest of the story.

Clearpack (www.clearpack.com) is a Singapore based technical powerhouse and a world leader in designing and deploying custom Packaging machines for various FMCG industries with one of its factories in Greater Noida in India.

PARC (www.atmanirbhar.org), Pilani AtmaNirbhar Resource Center, is a social incubator and service provider with the mission of community development in and around Pilani, Rajasthan.

So, what do these two organizations have in common? Nothing, except that both were founded and are run by BITS Pilani alumni. And that’s the spark that lit a fire!

During the COVID-19 lockdown period when the need for low cost, rapidly designed, stripped down ventilators became apparent, these two powerhouses got together to take on the task online. Led brilliantly by Clearpack CEO, Govind Bhandari, a geographically dispersed team was quickly assembled to complement the talented Clearpack workforce. Four doctors to advise on the clinical aspects, a ventilator design veteran (also a BITS alumnus) to advise on the technical aspects, a medical electronics expert from Italy and various others to fill the different roles required.

In addition to the technical challenges, there were some unique challenges posed by the lockdown orders in force. Factories were closed, technical teams could not meet face-to-face, important components could not be sourced etc. This is where the vast BITS alumni network came into play. All these issues were tackled and resolved on a war footing by connecting with different alumni who were uniquely qualified for the task at hand. We must also acknowledge the facilitation by the Greater Noida authorities which was crucial to this project.

The result is that today we have a working prototype of RESPIMATIC 100, a low-cost emergency respirator system that has been undergoing stress tests continuously for the past number of days. And holding up extremely well! The RESPIMATIC 100 is designed to fit in the huge capability gap between a hand powered Ambu bag and a full featured ventilator. There are many situations where a complex ventilator is either unavailable or unaffordable but a hand powered Ambu bag falls way short of the need. The need for such a device will continue well beyond the current COVID-19 pandemic especially in remote parts of India.

No doubt it will need refinement as it undergoes tests. No doubt that the certification process is arduous. No doubt there will be unexpected challenges. But that is what this team is geared up for. All in all, a great example of what large hearts, determination and collaboration can do.

PARC Plantation Drive

Sunil Nanda
March 26, 2020

Last semester, Pilani AtmaNirbhar Resource Centre (PARC) collaborated with Team BITS at Shell-Eco marathon, to plant a 100 fruit bearing trees in and around Pilani. The entire drive was led by Team AtmaNirbhar (Student Division of PARC) with the help of Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Churu. A local farmer was identified and entrusted with fifty of these newly planted trees, which will go on to help supplement his annual income. The rest have been placed under the care of the Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Churu.


The income generated from these fruit bearing trees will further be used to kick-start other such profitable venture bearing in mind the best interests of the farmers in and around Pilani. This not only makes our farmers self-sufficient but also takes us one step closer to a greener environment.

We would like to thank Dr. Jola Dubey and Yatik Gupta (Manager, Team Bits) for their integral role in this fruitful endeavour.

Preventing Blindness - Eye Camp at PARC

Sunil Nanda
March 11, 2020

March 7, 2020

Continuing with our series of health camps, a free eye camp was held at PARC in partnership with BITS, District Police Jhunjunu, Mahadev Scindia Eye Hospital And District Blindness Prevention Society Jhunjunu. The doctors who offered their valuable time and expertise were Dr. P. K. Sehgal and Dr. Ankit. The Chief Guest was Mr. Jagdish Sharma, IPS, Jhunjunu.

Following an eye exam for all patients, some were provided with medicines, some with eyeglasses and some others were short-listed for cataract surgery which will be scheduled at the hospital soon.

Special emphasis was given to taxi drivers, school bus drivers and long-distance bus drivers. At the end of the day Mr. Jagdish Sharma administered an oath for all drivers for safe driving.

It was the biggest health camp organized by PARC so far.

Number of Patients attended – 476

Number of patients given glasses – 92

Number of patients given medicines – 278

Number of minor surgeries performed on the spot - 5

Number recommended for hospital surgery – 63

Included in the patients

Taxi drivers – 70

Long distance Bus Drivers – 70

Rest were residents from surrounding areas

Young men who found PACE in their lives

Sunil Nanda
February 23, 2020

In the evenings, Ravi Kumar used to aimlessly wander the lanes of his village, Hanumantpura (Jhunjhunu District). An 8th standard school drop-out, Ravi did work in the fields, but would earn just Rs 150 for a hard day. With that, he had to support his parents and two brothers. In short, he was not unlike millions of other young men in this country, under-educated, under- or unemployed, and in fact unemployable.

Yet today he works in a five-star hotel in Shirdi. With food and accommodation covered, he is able to actually save some of his income and send it home.

Anil Meena is from a small village called Shimla, also near Jhunjhunu. With seven children, Anil’s father had a hard time caring for the family on his income from a stone mine factory. So as a young boy, Anil had to find work to bring the family a little more money; for Rs 1500 a month, he performed poojas at the local temple. After graduating from school, he joined a cable TV network for Rs 3500 per month. But by then he was an alcoholic and his family had lost all respect for him.

Yet today he works at a hotel in Jodhpur, earning Rs 5000 per month.

Starting at 18, Lal Singh worked as construction labour for two years, the only earner for his aging parents and two siblings. Then he actually started a business on his own and earned what he calls a “lot of money”. But as he also says, the money “all was thrown away and the God was not happy with me.”

Yet today he works at a hotel in Qatar, earning Rs 50,000 per month.

These are just three stories of many more that have emerged from Pratham’s PACE at the PARC in Pilani. Each of these young men heard, sometimes almost by chance, of the 3 month hospitality course PACE offered and signed up. Sometimes they didn’t enjoy the programme, or found everything strange and different, or they struggled. But all of them finished the course. And with the training and skills they learned at PACE, they were able to get jobs they could not even have dreamed of before. Ravi Kumar said it best — the Shirdi hotel was the first five-star hotel he had ever seen.

For these three men, and for Bantu Lal Meghwal, Ikendra Yadav, Ravindra Singh, Jitendra Jagir and many more, PACE has transformed their lives in ways that seem astounding even to them. And they tell us these things. Some speak of their “best reward” being not the money, but words of praise from bosses. “I like your work”, Anil heard from his manager. “You have been a valuable, member of our team and we have noticed all the hard work you have put in, keep it up” is what Ikendra heard. Bantu Lal says: “I believe life is filled with lot of challenges [but] whoever tackles it fearlessly can sail towards success.” Jitendra speaks simply of how he “gave my 100% while at the Centre”. Ravindra Singh learned the importance of “personalized service”, because “everyone is unique.” To Ravi Kumar, it meant a lot that he could help financially in building his family’s house: he now has the respect of his family and his neighbours.

In what they tell us about dreams and mobility, learning and achievement, there is something extraordinarily moving in all these stories. In the years to come, no doubt there will be plenty more such young men and women who come through the PACE programme at the Pilani Atmanirbhar Resource Center to tell us more such stories.

-Dilip D’souza

PARC spearheads Pilani road upgradation!

Sunil Nanda
January 26, 2020

Visitors to BITS Pilani would have noticed and lamented the sad state of the main road that leads to the campus gates. It was in shambles.

Well, Dr. Jola Dubey of PARC (Pilani AtmaNirbhar Resource Center) took up the challenge under the guidance of PARC Director, Dr. Vinod Dube. She wrote to the PM, the Transport Minister and the local Municipal authorities explaining, with documentary evidence, the pathetic state of the road and demanded action.

Lo and Behold, she was heard and acknowledged and today the road work has begun. So, the next time you visit Pilani you know who to thank for a far smoother ride into the city.

Congratulations to the team!
